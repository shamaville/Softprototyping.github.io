Modular Patterns and Laser Cutting

Step 1

Firstly, I made a hand sketch of my concept. I then developed the sketch in Adobe Illustrator. Also the lines of your designs must be saved as 0.0001. After saving the file, you will have to export it as a 'DXF' file so that Rhino can read the file.

Modular Patterns and Laser Cutting

Step 2

Once in Rhino, you have to adjust the file so it is in full view, then you click on the print function. After making a test run of the various speed, frequency and power settings, you have to choose the appropriate one that suits your material.

Modular Patterns and Laser Cutting

Step 3

Once the Laser Cutter is turned on you have to manually adjust it. To do this you will press button 8. Once X/Y is disabled you can then move the laser sensor manually.

Modular Patterns and Laser Cutting

Step 4

You then move the laser head to indicate the starting point for cutting. Also the metal prong is brought down so as to determine the distance between the cutter and the material that is to be cut. Ideally, the prong should touch the surface of the material without any resistance.

Modular Patterns and Laser Cutting

Step 5

The vaccum suction most be turned on before the laser cutter can begin cutting. I had to use two different materials to give contrast to my work. This makes the patterns clearly visible.
